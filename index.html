Why Study Physics?

Physics is the fundamental science that explains how the universe behaves. From the motion of planets to the behavior of subatomic particles, physics provides the framework that connects all aspects of the physical world.

Classical Physics

Classical physics deals with the physics of everyday life and observable phenomena. It primarily focuses on Newton's laws of motion and classical mechanics, explaining forces, motion, and energy.

Relativistic Physics

Relativistic physics, introduced by Albert Einstein, expands on classical physics and includes the study of phenomena that occur at speeds close to the speed of light. This area primarily involves special and general relativity.
